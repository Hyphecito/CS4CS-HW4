SDLC Methodologies

I will write a paragraph for each methodology of SDLC. There are many of them but I will suggest a few. My suggestions and their reasons are as follows:

  • Waterfall: It is a simple and structured method, however it has limited feedback so may be risky for long projects.
  • Incremental: Early to deliver key features and very flexible, however needs complex planning and may face integration problems.
  • Spiral: Good risk management and good feedback possibility, however complex and costly.

Among so many SDLC methodologies the ones that are in AGILE classification are better. Among AGILE SDLC Methods, I also have a few suggestions:

  • Scrum: Uses sprints which are meetings generally made bi-weekly. Good for changing requirements.
  • Kanban: Good with visulazing tasks, focuses on continuous delivery.
  • XP: Uses pair programming and frequent releases. Good for changing requirements.
